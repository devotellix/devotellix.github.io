Frequently Asked Questions

How does the dilution calculator calculate ratios?

AeroRatio uses a physics-based model that takes into account the paint's base viscosity coefficient, the diameter of your airbrush nozzle, the air pressure (PSI), the desired painting effect (e.g. primer, basecoat, glaze), and ambient evaporation factors (temperature and humidity) to suggest the optimal drops of paint, thinner, and flow improver.

Which paint brands are supported?

AeroRatio comes with a preloaded catalog of over 1,500 paints from wargaming and scale modeling brands, including Citadel (Base, Layer, Air, Contrast, Shade, etc.), Vallejo (Model Color, Game Color, Model Air, etc.), The Army Painter, Pro Acryl, Scale75, and Tamiya. You can also create your own custom paint profiles with specific viscosity ratings.

How do I manage my saved recipes?

All your saved recipes are located in the Journal tab. You can edit recipe names, add notes, select a photo, scale the quantities for large painting sessions, or export the recipe as an A4 PDF sheet for printing.

What are multi-layer sequences?

In the Journal tab, you can build paint sequences by stacking multiple recipes (up to 5 steps, e.g. Apprêt/Primer -> Basecoat -> Highlight -> Varnish). This allows you to document your entire painting workflow for a project or miniature squad.

What is the Troubleshooting Diagnostics tree?

If you encounter airbrushing issues such as tip dry, clogging, orange peel, splattering, pooling, or spiderwebbing, go to the Diagnostics tab. It provides an interactive step-by-step diagnostic tree that helps you identify the symptoms and suggests corrective actions.

Are there any ads or subscription fees?

No. AeroRatio is a premium, paid upfront application. There are zero ads, subscription fees, or features hidden behind paywalls. Every single update is fully included with the initial purchase.
