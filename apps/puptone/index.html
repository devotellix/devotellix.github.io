Usage Guidelines

Safe High-Frequency Training

Protecting your dog's hearing and building positive habits.

1. Use Short Bursts

Dogs have highly sensitive hearing. High-frequency tones should be played in brief 1-to-2 second pulses. Avoid keeping the whistle active for prolonged intervals.

2. Low Volume Initial Calibration

Start your training sessions at lower volumes and check your dog's reactions. Keep the volume output appropriate for the distance and noise level.

3. Bluetooth Delay Warning

Wireless earphones or speakers introduce up to 300ms of audio latency. For reward timing that coordinates with the clicker, use the built-in speaker.
