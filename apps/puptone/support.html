Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the whistle sound quiet or silent to me?

Human adult hearing ranges up to 15-20 kHz and diminishes with age, whereas dogs can perceive high-pitched frequencies up to 45 kHz. To confirm your device is generating audio correctly, drop the slider down to 1-2 kHz (which is fully audible to humans).

Is it normal for my dog to ignore the whistle at first?

Yes. A whistle is not a remote control; it is a cue. You must pair the whistle sound with a reward (such as high-value treats) so your dog associates the tone with positive reinforcement. Use the **Frequency Finder** to verify which frequency gets their immediate attention.

Are high-frequency sounds safe for my dog?

Yes, if used responsibly. High-frequency whistles should be used as brief 1-to-2 second signals. Avoid continuous play, and never play high-frequency sounds at close range or high volumes directly into your pet's ears. Stop immediately if your dog displays signs of distress or agitation.
