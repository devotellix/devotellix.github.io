The Science Behind Lateral Inhibition

Tonal tinnitus is frequently caused by damaged hair cells in the inner ear. Because these cells stop transmitting signals, the corresponding neurons in the brain's auditory cortex receive no input, causing them to hyper-oscillate and manifest as a ringing sound.

Acoustic Notched-Noise Therapy plays broadband sound containing all frequencies except the user's specific tinnitus frequency. By doing this:

  • Neighbor Stimulation: Sound frequencies directly adjacent to your tinnitus are stimulated normally.
  • Synaptic Suppression: The active surrounding neurons send inhibitory neural links back to the hyperactive zone (lateral inhibition).
  • Habituation Progress: With regular listening, the hyperactive neurons calm down, reducing the loudness of the perceived ringing over time.
